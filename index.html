Executive Summary

Industrial and logistics occupiers in EMEA are moving from broad based expansion to more targeted growth in core, high quality locations as supply and demand rebalance. Softer overall take up and slightly higher vacancy are offset by persistent shortages of modern, ESG compliant space in prime hubs, which sustains rental growth and forces more deliberate footprint choices.

These conditions are prompting occupiers to pay for quality, commit earlier to future capacity, and consolidate activity into scalable platforms that can support automation and net zero ambitions.

Lease management needs to integrate network design, ESG standards, automation plans, and labour dynamics rather than treat leases as isolated contracts, prioritizing early planning of renewals, pre leasing in critical hubs, and proactive migration away from substandard sites.

Key Themes

1
Expansion signals recentering on core distribution platforms

Aggregate take-up remains historically high, concentrating in core markets where occupiers expect structural demand to endure. Many tenants intend to expand and are making large new and future leases, indicating a strategic recentering on key distribution platforms rather than broad based expansion. Lease teams should prioritize scenario planning around core hubs and manage legacy peripheral sites accordingly.

2
Preference for modern and sustainable space reshapes lease quality thresholds

Demand is strongest for modern, ESG compliant, energy-efficient space, with occupiers prepared to pay higher rents and incorporating advanced automation and sustainability features. Lease teams need to identify buildings meeting these criteria and manage renewals, relocations, and green lease clauses to migrate operations into higher quality assets.

3
Tight prime vacancy and selective flexibility increase value of forward commitments

Structural undersupply and very low vacancy in prime hubs sustain rental growth and limit immediate availability, prompting occupiers to sign large pre-leases and build-to-suit commitments locking in future capacity. Lease managers should integrate these forward commitments into portfolio and cash flow planning to ensure smooth operational transitions.

Market Analysis for Occupiers

Industrial logistics occupier demand across EMEA softened slightly in 2024 but remains historically elevated, with vacancy low in prime hubs due to scarce new supply.

Rental growth continues, driven by willingness to pay for modern, energy-efficient, and ESG-compliant facilities, causing landlords to renovate existing stock.

Nearshoring and supply chain de-risking boost demand from manufacturing, EV, and pharmaceuticals, while survey data indicate occupiers prioritize labour availability and sustainability in site selection.

Investment volumes in the sector surged, with developers focusing on brownfield regeneration and sustainable building certifications.
